You know your colours.

You already know your colour energies. Your colleagues know theirs. The hard part was never the knowledge. It is remembering to use it in the moment, in the meeting, with the actual person in front of you.

Step one

Find it in seconds

Insights Discovery sits on the left rail in Teams, the same place you reach for chat or calendar. One click and you are in.

That's the one

The circular colour mark on the rail. Pin it so it's always there, then it becomes a glance, not a hunt. Click it and your dashboard opens inside Teams.

Tip for your learners: if it isn't pinned, it lives under the three dots. Pinning it is the single best habit you can build today.

Step three, the heart of it

A real meeting, thought through

You're chairing a project kick-off in Teams. Six people. Here is the same situation, used well in three moves.

This meeting's colour mix
Fiery Red Sunshine Yellow Blue Grn

Two minutes before, you open the meeting and read the mix. It skews loud and fast: Fiery Red and Sunshine Yellow lead. Your data lead leads strongly with Cool Blue, your operations colleague more quietly with Earth Green. Straight away you know the two people holding the detail and the delivery realism are the ones most likely to be talked over.

Data lead
Check the do's and don'ts: give the rationale and a moment to think, don't put her on the spot.
Operations
Plan to bring him in by name. He goes quiet under pressure, not because he has nothing.
R
Let those who lead with Fiery Red set the pace. That energy gets a plan locked quickly, which is a strength, not a problem to manage.
B
Deliberately carve a moment: "I'd like your read on the data risks, take a minute, no need to answer instantly." That one sentence is the do's and don'ts used live.
Y
Give the colleague who leads with Sunshine Yellow a clear, recognised slot for ideas, then steer back to actions rather than shutting the energy down.
G
When it turns combative, bring the colleague who leads with Earth Green in by name and acknowledge the pressure the change puts on his team.

You follow up in the styles that land. Those who lead with Fiery Red get a three line action summary. Your data lead gets the detailed written rationale she can pick apart at her own pace. That is the weekly habit in miniature: small, repeated, in the flow of work.

None of this is new knowledge. The app just removes the excuse of "I forgot who's who" by putting it right there in the meeting.
